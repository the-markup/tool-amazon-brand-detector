Amazon Brand Detector

About

The Markup built this extension to reveal Amazon brands and exclusives while you shop online.

Amazon has registered trademarks for over 150 private-label brands and sells hundreds of thousands of products from these brands, most of which are not obviously owned by Amazon. We found that Amazon did not disclose their ownership for almost 40 percent of Amazon brands and exclusives in popular searches, and few Americans can identify top-selling Amazon brands. We also found Amazon gives itself a leg up in search, placing its own brands ahead of better-rated competitors.

Learn more about the story behind these numbers at The Markup.

Identification Methods

This extension looks for several clues to highlight products as Amazon.

"Featured from our brands"
Amazon labels some items "Featured from Our Brands." The items were tagged “sponsored” in the source code but not publicly. Amazon says these are neither ads nor search results, but rather "merchandising placement."

Carousel
In about three in four popular search pages, Amazon added special sections in of the search results page devoted to Amazon's brands and exclusives. We call these carousels.

“Our brands” Filter
Amazon describes "our brands" as "a collection of Amazon private brands and a curated selection of brands sold exclusively on Amazon." We access the filter in the background using an API call.

Proprietary Electronics
Identified from the Amazon Best-Sellers list for "Amazon Devices & Accessories," or added manually. Includes Ring doorbells, Fire TVs, and Kindles.

Known Brands and Other Disclaimers
We look for obvious brand names like “AmazonBasics” and or a disclosure in the title or listing that it’s an “Amazon brand.” See more on GitHub.
