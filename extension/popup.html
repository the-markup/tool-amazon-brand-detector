Amazon Brand Detector

About

The Markup built this extension to identify Amazon brands and exclusives while you shop online.

Amazon has registered trademarks for more than 150 private-label brands and sells hundreds of thousands of products under those names, most of which are not obviously affiliated with Amazon. We found that Amazon did not disclose its connection to for almost 40 percent of Amazon brands and exclusive items in popular searches, and few Americans can identify even top-selling Amazon brands as owned by Amazon.

We also found Amazon gives itself a leg up in search, placing products from its own brands and exclusives ahead of better-rated competitors.

Learn more about the story behind these numbers at The Markup.

Identification Methods

This extension looks for several clues to identify products as Amazon, but may still miss some.

"Featured from our brands"
Amazon labels some items "Featured from our brands." The items were tagged “Sponsored” in the source code but not publicly. Amazon says these are neither ads nor search results, but rather "merchandising placement."

Carousel
In about three in four popular search pages, Amazon added special sections devoted to Amazon brands and exclusives to the search results page. We call these carousels.

“Our Brands” Filter
Amazon describes "Our Brands" as "a collection of Amazon private brands and a curated selection of brands sold exclusively on Amazon." We access the filter in the background using an API call.

Proprietary Electronics
Identified from the Amazon Best Sellers list for "Amazon Devices & Accessories," or added manually. Includes Ring doorbells, Fire TVs, and Kindles.

Known Brands and Other Disclaimers
We look for obvious brand names like “AmazonBasics” and/or a disclosure in the title or listing that it’s an “Amazon Brand.” See more on GitHub.
