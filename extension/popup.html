Amazon Brand Detector

About

The Markup built this extension to reveal Amazon brands and exclusives while you shop online.

Amazon has trademarked over 150 private-label brands and sells hundreds of thousands of products from these brands. Almost 40 percent of Amazon brands and exclusives in popular searches were not disclosed to shoppers, and few Americans can identify top-selling Amazon brands. Amazon gives itself an unfair advantage in search, placing its own brands ahead of better-rated competitors.

Learn more about the story behind these numbers at The Markup.

Identification Methods

This extension looks for several clues to highlight products as Amazon.

"Featured from our brands"
Labelled "Featured from Our Brands." Source code suggests these products are ads, but Amazon says they are neither an ad nor a search result, but rather "merchandising placement."

Carousel
Identified in a carousel devoted to Amazon's brands and exclusives. Carousels are present in about three in four of the popular search pages we analyzed.

“Our brands” Filter
Amazon describes "our brands" as "a collection of Amazon private brands and a curated selection of brands sold exclusively on Amazon." We access the filter in the background using an API call.

Proprietary Electronics
Identified from the Amazon Best-Sellers list for "Amazon Devices & Accessories," or added manually. Made up of electronics like Ring doorbells, Fire TVs, and Kindles.

Known Brands and Other Disclaimers
Brand names like “AmazonBasics” and disclaimers like “Amazon brand.” See the dislaimers and brands we used on GitHub.
